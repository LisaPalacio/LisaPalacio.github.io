My name is Lisa Palacio, and I am currently a fourth-year psychology major at the University of California, Riverside. My journey into the world of psychology began with a profound curiosity about human behavior and the complex workings of the brain. This passion has shaped my academic and professional path, motivating me to delve deeply into the various facets of psychology. Throughout my time at university, I have immersed myself in a diverse array of coursework that has enriched my understanding of psychological theories and practices. Classes such as cognitive psychology, neuropsychology, and behavioral psychology have been particularly impactful. These courses have not only provided me with foundational knowledge but also inspired me to explore the intricate connections between mental processes and human behavior. I am fascinated by how our thoughts, emotions, and actions are interconnected, and this interest drives my desire to uncover more about the brain's mechanisms. In addition to my academic pursuits, I have actively sought out practical experiences to complement my learning. I am eager to gain further practical experience through internships that will prepare me for graduate studies. My ultimate goal is to become a licensed psychologist, specializing in neuropsychology, where I can combine my interests in research and patient care to make a meaningful impact in the lives of others. Outside of my academic life, I enjoy reading, hiking, and exploring new ideas through podcasts and documentaries. I believe that a well-rounded perspective enhances my understanding of psychology and human behavior. Engaging with diverse viewpoints allows me to approach psychological concepts with an open mind, which I find invaluable. I am committed to lifelong learning and look forward to the journey ahead as I pursue my passion for psychology and the study of the brain. With each new experience, I am reminded of the profound impact that understanding the mind can have on individuals and society as a whole.
I have participated in several research projects focused on cognitive functioning and memory retention. Collaborating with professors and fellow students has given me invaluable hands-on experience in data collection and analysis, reinforcing my analytical skills and deepening my commitment to the field of psychology. One particular project that stands out involved examining how different learning techniques affect memory recall, which allowed me to witness firsthand the application of psychological principles in a research setting. As I continue my studies, I find myself increasingly drawn to the field of clinical neuropsychology. My interest lies in understanding how brain function impacts behavior and emotional well-being. I am eager to learn more about neurological disorders and the ways in which psychological interventions can improve patients' quality of life. I believe that a solid understanding of the brain's workings is essential for any psychologist, and I am committed to expanding my knowledge in this area.
